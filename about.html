03 : About me

Short bio

Hello! I'm Patryk Nienaltowski, currently pursuing a PhD at ETH Zürich's Institute of Environmental Engineering in collaboration with Lyncee Tec, Lausanne. I am privileged to work under the supervision of Roman Stocker and Yves Emery. My academic voyage began at the Warsaw University of Technology where, mentored by Malgorzata Kujawinska, I earned my Master's in Mechatronics.

I am currently focusing on the use of digital holographic microscopy (DHM) for advanced 3D tracking of microorganisms. In this pursuit, I am developing an algorithm designed for efficient processing of large sampling volumes while ensuring high precision and broad applicability. Preliminary findings indicate the potential of this method in three distinct applications: 3D tracking of bacterial motility, analysis of phytoplankton-bacteria interactions, and 3D observation of microbial flagella morphology. This research holds promise for shedding new light on the microcosmos that often goes unseen.

I'm also involved with the PHYMOT network, a European consortium of universities, research institutes, and industrial partners focusing on the physics of microbial motility.

Research Focus

3D tracking of microbes, quantitative phase imaging, bacteria-phytoplankton interactions, marine microbiology, 3D flagella tracing, image processing
